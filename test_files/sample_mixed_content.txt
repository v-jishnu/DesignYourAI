=== DESCRIPTIVE Q&A FORMAT ===

Q: What is the vanishing gradient problem in deep learning?
A: The vanishing gradient problem occurs when gradients become extremely small during backpropagation through deep networks, making it difficult for early layers to learn. This happens because gradients are multiplied repeatedly through each layer, and if they're less than 1, they exponentially decrease. Solutions include using activation functions like ReLU, batch normalization, or skip connections.

Q: What is the difference between bagging and boosting?
A: Bagging (Bootstrap Aggregating) trains multiple models independently on different subsets of data and combines their predictions through voting or averaging. Boosting trains models sequentially, where each new model focuses on correcting the errors of previous models. Bagging reduces variance, while boosting reduces bias.

Question: What is the purpose of dropout in neural networks?
Answer: Dropout is a regularization technique that randomly deactivates a fraction of neurons during training. This prevents the network from becoming too dependent on specific neurons and encourages more robust feature learning. During inference, all neurons are active but their outputs are scaled to account for the dropout rate used during training.
